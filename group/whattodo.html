    Foster!

    Fostering can be a great way to have sheltered and abandon pets socialize before their next adoption as well as get the most individual care they need.

    Fostering is a direct and positive way to give one-on-one attention to an animal in need. It can help foster a nuturing and unique amount og attention to those who have been abandoned or lost.

    Plus, fostering can help open more space for incoming animals that can't find a home just yet. Help open some space by fostering one today!

    Report Animal Cruelty

    If you know someone is abusing an animal, speak up by reporting your suspicions to the humane organization, an animal control agendy, or your local law enforcement agency.

    Call 311 to report animal cruelty in the New York City area. Call 911 to report crimes in progress.

    How to Report Cruelty

  • Write a factual and clear statement of your observation with a time and date stamp. Be as concise as possible. Keep a log when needed to file a report
  • Photographs/videos in the environment found.Note:DON'T PUT YOURSELF IN DANGER. Be cautious and never enter one's property without permission.
  • Have contact information with law enforments or professionals with factual information regarding understanding on how to react to an abusive situation

    • Signs of Cruelty

  • Fur infested with fleas, ticks, etc.
  • Neglet of grooming needs: Overgrown nails, dirty and/or extreme matted fur, heavy discharge from eyes or nose.
  • Noticable aggression toward pet by owner in terms physical agression or abuse
  • Weak limbs, bony features in the abdomen area. Ribs are clearly shown.

    • It's not just physical

    Physical signs of abuse isn't always the only sign.

  • Pets tied up alone for a long period of time with no change of food or water.
  • Unsanitary cages, kennels, or sleeping areas stained with feces or urine.
  • Inadequate amount of space for normal movement of sleeping, sitting, or standing
  • Kept outside in harsh weather without proper shelter, food, and water.
